The Glider

Date: 2024–2025 · Role: Firmware & CAD

Overview

This project involved a lot of learning. The goal was to make a foam glider that could be remotely controlled with two ESP32s to hit a specific target. We were not allowed to use propellers because of school policy. This was quite a challenge because I had not done remote control between two microcontrollers before, and I had also never designed a plane. I had to learn about airfoil design as well as wireless communication protocols. I successfully made a glider with turning controlled by two underwing servos, and won first place in our staff competition.

My Planes Downfall (literally)

The downfall of my plane came from its heavy weight and lack of lift. Unfortunately, competing in the staff competition made it clear that servo-controlled gliders without a propeller would be a very hard beginner electronics competition. My glider did the best, but no one’s glider really “glided,” so unfortunately it was decided there would be no public student-run version of this competition.
