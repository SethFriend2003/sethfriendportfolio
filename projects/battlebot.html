Battle Bots!

Date: 2024 & 2025 · Role: Firmware & CAD

Overview

This was our second year competing in the Robo Rumble here at UT. Both years I made a verticle spinner robot, with much more success the second year. I designed the chassi as well as the electrical system, and we used a premade weapon. We chose TPU for the whole chassi for its high energy absorbtion and impact resistance. Compared to our first year we actually almost had a whole extra pound of weight, and added an extra layer of TPU armor. We made it to quarter finals, and I belive we would have made it further if we didn't have a weapon mount design flaw.

The Weapon

Oue downfall was our weapon mount, as we were using the barrel of the motor to power the weapon. Our issue was the end of the motor was spinning in a small bearing pressed into a TPU upright. This worked fine in testing, but against actualy robots, the TPU upright would bend, and the motor would pop out. If we were to do it again, I would make the upright out of a more rigid material, to prevent this from happening.
